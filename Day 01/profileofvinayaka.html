Lord Ganesha

Lord Vinaya, also known as Lord Ganesha or Vinayaka, is a revered Hindu deity considered the remover of obstacles, the god of new beginnings, wisdom, and prosperity, often worshipped before starting any important undertaking; he is depicted with an elephant head, large ears, a small mouse as his vehicle, and is known for his association with knowledge and auspicious beginnings. Key points about Lord Vinaya: Identity: A prominent figure in the Hindu pantheon, primarily recognized as the son of Shiva and Parvati. Symbolism: Elephant head: Represents wisdom and the ability to see beyond limitations. Broken tusk: Symbolizes the idea of overcoming ego and prioritizing intellect. Mouse as vehicle: Represents the ability to control even the smallest of obstacles. Modak (sweet): Represents the enjoyment of life's pleasures. Attributes: Removal of obstacles: Considered the primary function of Lord Vinaya, often invoked to overcome challenges in life. Knowledge and wisdom: Associated with learning, intellect, and the pursuit of knowledge. Auspicious beginnings: Traditionally worshipped at the start of new ventures to ensure success. Festivals: Ganesh Chaturthi: The major festival dedicated to Lord Ganesha, where idols are worshipped and then immersed in water. Other names: Ganapati, Gajanana, Lambodara.
